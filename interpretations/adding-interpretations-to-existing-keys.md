# Adding Interpretations to existing keys
The vast majority of determinations derive from taxonomic keys, by people who do not have an in-depth understanding of taxonomy or nomenclature. These people are typically follow the key and write the name that is at the end. In order to minimise disruption, it is desirable to convert existing keys to provide Interpretations. To do this, a taxon expert must assess each key and decide which Interpretation the author of the key is using. In a dichotomous key, each taxon only appears once, and so has a direct one-to-one relationship to an Interpretation. Keys where taxa appear multiple times may be far more complex, as each appearance of a taxon may relate to a different Interpretation.

Once each taxon result has been matched to an Interpretation, a list of those matches may be released for others to use. This list may be kept separate or pencilled into the key directly.
